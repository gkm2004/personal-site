I'm a computer science student interested in how data and systems shape how we see the world. I'm especially drawn to questions around place, public information, and how technical tools are used in real contexts.

I enjoy working alongside other students and early-stage collaborators, especially in spaces where learning together matters more than polish or speed. I value clear communication, shared credit, and being honest about what I don't yet know.

I keep a Binder of Thoughts on this site as a way to think in public : notes, reflections, and ideas in progress. It's not meant to be definitive, just a record of how my thinking evolves over time.

Right now, I'm focused on learning, building, and working on projects that feel thoughtful and grounded. If something here resonates with you, feel free to reach out.

Who this is for

This page is mainly for fellow students who are working on:

  • course projects
  • research ideas
  • hackathon or side projects
  • data-for-good or public-interest work

I'm also open to thoughtful, small-scope collaborations beyond student spaces when expectations are clear and aligned.

What collaboration looks like

When I collaborate, I value:

  • shared learning
  • clear communication
  • small, well-defined scope
  • documenting as we go
  • shared credit

If this sounds like something you'd enjoy, feel free to reach out and tell me what you're working on and what kind of collaboration you're hoping for.
