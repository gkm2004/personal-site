The question

I wanted to explore how crime data can be made more understandable and grounded in place. As someone who enjoys crime podcasts and is curious about how crime is discussed in the abstract, I was interested in seeing what the data for my own city actually looks like when visualized. This project was an opportunity to work with public crime data and experiment with maps and interactive charts.

Approach

  • I would like to acknowledge that all data has been taken from the official website of the Regina police and can be viewed here.
  • I visualized crime stats in Regina based on different areas.

Output

What I learned

  • I learned how data scope directly affects interpretation. Even well-designed maps and charts can suggest patterns that aren’t statistically meaningful when the time window is too short.

Next steps

  • The current dataset includes one month of reported crime. As a result, the dashboard is exploratory and not intended for trend analysis.
  • Rather than extending this dashboard immediately, a more meaningful next step would be a follow-up project using a longer time window. With multiple months of data, the focus could shift from exploration to identifying stable spatial and temporal patterns, and evaluating whether observed variations persist over time.
